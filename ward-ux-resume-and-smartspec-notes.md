## SmartSpec 2.0 – Full Case Study (portfolio narrative)

### Overview

Caldwell’s SmartSpec is an online product configurator that helps distributors and end customers quickly specify custom below-the-hook lifting devices. In 2024–2025 SmartSpec 2.0 launched as a major UX, UI, and workflow upgrade that made it dramatically faster and easier to configure custom equipment and get quotes and drawings.

- **Role**: UI/UX Designer & Usability Lead  
- **Company**: The Caldwell Group, Inc.  
- **Timeline**: 2024–2025  
- **Users**: Rigging distributors, crane operators, construction managers, and their customers  
- **Key tasks**:
  - Configure **custom lifting beams**
  - Request **quotes and engineering drawings**
  - Configure **Dura-Mod** (modular spreader system) builds  
- **Outcomes**:
  - **~60% increase in SmartSpec usage** within the first week after SmartSpec 2.0 launch (Google Analytics), sustained over time  
  - Estimated **10+ hours/month saved per salesperson** on custom specs  
  - Turnaround time for quotes/drawings reduced from **days or a week+ to minutes**  
  - Strong positive feedback on the **new look & feel**, clarity, and ease of use  

External context:

- [Get Custom Equipment Quotes Faster with Improved SmartSpec Tool](https://www.caldwellinc.com/blog/get-custom-equipment-quotes-faster-with-an-improved-smartspec-tool)  
- [The SmartSpec Revolution and Reboot](https://www.caldwellinc.com/blog/the-smart-spec-revolution-and-reboot)

### Problem

Before SmartSpec 2.0:

- **Slow, manual quoting**: Every custom tweak (capacity, spans, attachments) required manual recalculation, engineering review, and back-and-forth to generate drawings and pricing. It often took **days to a week or more** to get a complete quote package, creating friction and risking lost deals.
- **Complex, error-prone configuration**: Users had to understand both lifting products and engineering constraints. The original interface exposed complexity, leaving room for misinterpretation and errors.
- **Limited access**: Historically, only registered users could use SmartSpec, which made it harder for distributors to hand customers a link and let them explore scenarios early in the process.
- **Internal workload**: Sales and engineering teams spent significant time translating customer intent into valid specs, checking calculations, and coordinating drawings.

### Goals & success criteria

- Make complex configuration feel simple and guided for both technical and non-technical users.
- Increase SmartSpec adoption and usage (Google Analytics).
- Shorten time-to-quote and drawings from days/weeks to minutes.
- Expand access beyond registered users to support discovery and distributor–customer collaboration.
- Reduce manual back-office work by automating repeatable steps and ensuring specs are right the first time.

### My role

As **UI/UX Designer & Usability Lead**, responsibilities included:

- UX strategy and end-to-end interaction design for SmartSpec 2.0.
- Translating complex engineering constraints into simple, guided flows.
- Designing the step-by-step configuration experience for custom lifting beams, Dura-Mod builds, and other lifter models.
- Planning and facilitating usability feedback cycles with internal stakeholders and external users.
- Helping define requirements for automation and workflow changes that reduced manual effort in the quoting process.
- Collaborating with marketing and leadership to ensure the UI and content supported Caldwell’s brand promise of innovation and reliability.

### Key design decisions

#### Guided, step-by-step configuration

- Reorganized configuration into a **logical progression** that walks users from high-level intent through detailed options:
  - Start with what users are lifting and how (beam type, Dura-Mod configuration, load, spans).
  - Progress through capacity, dimensions, and attachment options with guardrails that prevent invalid combinations.
  - Provide inline guidance and context so users can understand why certain options are available or restricted.
- Outcome: reduced guesswork, fewer wrong turns, and a smoother path for both first-time and experienced users.

#### Serving both experts and non-experts

- Designed the interface to work for:
  - Technical users (distributors and engineers who know exact load ratings and standards).
  - Non-technical users (customers who just know “I need to lift X safely.”)
- Techniques:
  - Clear labeling and grouping of options by real-world intent.
  - Defaults and suggested configurations to give non-experts a valid starting point.
  - Consistent layout and patterns across products to reduce re-learning.

#### Expanding access (anonymous usage)

- Introduced a version of SmartSpec that supports non-registered/anonymous users while still routing pricing and follow-up appropriately.
- Effects:
  - Distributors can share the URL directly with customers, who can explore configurations on their own.
  - Prospects searching for custom equipment can discover and try the tool without account friction, then be connected to the right distributor.

#### Automation behind the scenes

- Worked with engineering to remove manual steps from the quoting workflow:
  - Automating parts of the specification, calculation, and drawing/quote request handoff, so a valid configuration can move further without human intervention.
  - Ensuring the tool reflects the large number of engineering calculations required to keep configurations accurate and safe.
- For salespeople, this reduced manual re-entry of information and follow-up, saving an estimated **10+ hours per month** each on custom specs.

#### Visual refresh aligned with brand and clarity

- Focused on:
  - Cleaner layout and hierarchy for dense engineering information.
  - Visual consistency across lifter models and product lines.
  - Reinforcing Caldwell’s identity as a modern, innovative solutions provider.
- Feedback highlighted that the new look and feel felt more modern, professional, and easier to use, while still feeling like Caldwell.

### Results

- **Usage & adoption**
  - After SmartSpec 2.0 launched, usage increased by roughly **60% in the first week** based on Google Analytics data and stayed at that higher level over time.
  - Anonymous access increased first-time users and made it easier for distributors to integrate SmartSpec into their sales process.

- **Speed & efficiency**
  - The quoting pipeline moved from a process that “used to take days, sometimes a week or more” to one where quotes and detailed drawings can often be generated in **minutes** for many scenarios.
  - Salespeople saved an estimated **10+ hours per month** each on manual spec-ing and follow-up for custom orders.

- **Experience & brand**
  - Users described SmartSpec as faster, more intuitive, and a “game-changer” in how quickly they could respond to customers.
  - Internal stakeholders emphasized that the new SmartSpec “does the heavy lifting with the push of a button,” matching Caldwell’s innovation message.

### What I’d explore next

- Deeper analytics on flow drop-offs, task completion, and error patterns by segment (distributor vs end customer).
- More embedded guidance and example-driven starting templates for common lifting scenarios.
- Progressive disclosure of advanced options to avoid overwhelming non-experts.
- A structured usability research program with periodic tests across key user groups, including accessibility reviews.
